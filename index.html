Yasan Ding

Postdoctoral Researcher
School of Computer Science
Northwestern Polytechnical University

Yasan Ding is a postdoctoral researcher at the School of Computer Science, Northwestern Polytechnical University, P.R.China. He has conducted research in human-machine-thing crowd intelligence computing (CrowdHMT), social media data mining, and knowledge transfer. He earned his Bachelor's degree in Engineering in 2018 and his Ph.D. in Engineering in 2024 from Northwestern Polytechnical University, specializing in Internet of Things Engineering and Computer Science and Technology, respectively. In recent years, he has published over ten academic papers in high-level journals and conferences such as ACM CSUR, ACM TIST, ACM Ubicomp, IEEE COMST, and IEEE TMC. He holds three authorized invention patents. He received the ACM Xi'an Chapter Excellent Doctoral Dissertation Award in 2025 and the PCC 2024 Best Paper Award. He has led projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China Youth Science Fund and the China Postdoctoral General Project, and participated in a key project of the National Natural Science Foundation of China. He serves as the Session Chair for IEEE UIC '24 and as a reviewer for several international journals and conferences, including IEEE THMS, EAAI, and IP&M. He is a member of the CCF Committee on Ubiquitous Computing.
